My Story

My career began in Ghana as an ICT lecturer at Wa Polytechnic, teaching the fundamentals of the systems I would spend the next decade building. From there I moved into telecommunications and enterprise systems engineering — first with PAiC and Global Wavenet, then AMDOCS deploying BSS/OSS platforms across multiple countries, and later Huawei on large-scale telecom infrastructure. Every role kept pulling me deeper into one question: how do you keep complex, distributed systems both reliable and safe?

That question led me to South Africa as Operations Manager for an AWS cloud security team, leading engineers who hardened production environments at scale. Running a cloud security operation taught me that great security isn’t a bolt-on — it’s an engineering discipline that shows up in the architecture, the IAM model, the pipelines, and the day-to-day instincts of the people on the team.

Today I’m a graduate student at Baylor University’s Hankamer School of Business, pursuing an MS in Information Systems with a cybersecurity concentration. I’m focused on the place where applied AI and security meet: LLM-powered tooling, retrieval pipelines, and the guardrails that make them safe to ship. After I graduate in December 2026, I’m looking for cybersecurity and AI engineering roles where both halves of that practice matter.
